UI/UX Concept Breakdown — NGUNJUK Matcha Landing Page

This reference is a mobile-first editorial eCommerce landing page for a matcha drink brand. The visual direction mixes Japanese tea culture, fresh beverage lifestyle, soft wellness branding, and playful Gen Z product marketing. The page is designed as a long vertical scroll experience with strong hero imagery, large typography, curved marquee strips, lifestyle photography, and product call-to-actions.

1. Project Manager Perspective
Screen Purpose

The page is built to sell matcha as a lifestyle product, not just a drink. It introduces the brand, builds freshness and health credibility, highlights product flavors, and guides users toward ordering.

The main business goal is:

Convert visitors into buyers through emotional lifestyle appeal, freshness messaging, and repeated “Order Now” CTAs.

Product Context

This is likely for:

A matcha café
Ready-to-drink matcha brand
Online beverage ordering website
Social-media-driven drink brand
Mobile-first product landing page

The design feels optimized for Instagram/TikTok audiences, especially users who are attracted to aesthetic drinks, wellness products, and soft lifestyle branding.

User Intent

The user arriving on this page is expected to:

Understand the brand immediately.
Feel the drink is refreshing, premium, and healthy.
See the product visually in lifestyle situations.
Explore flavor options.
Order or subscribe to updates.

2. UI/UX Engineer Perspective
Overall Layout Structure

The page uses a single-column mobile landing page layout with multiple stacked full-width sections.

Approximate canvas behavior:

Viewport width: mobile-focused, around 390px–430px equivalent
Layout type: vertical scroll
Section rhythm: image-led blocks with editorial spacing
Background: warm cream base with dark green product sections
Border radius: large rounded corners on major image containers

The design has a mobile app-like flow, not a standard desktop website. Every section is composed to fit a narrow vertical screen.

3. Motion Graphics Developer Perspective
General Motion Direction

Motion should feel:

Smooth
Fresh
Light
Organic
Slightly playful
Premium but not overly corporate

Recommended easing:

cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)

This gives a soft editorial reveal.

Page Load Animation
Hero Load Sequence
Background sky image fades in.
Logo and menu slide down gently.
“Matcha” fades upward.
“On Perfect” fades in with slight delay.
Drink cups scale from 1.04 to 1.00.
CTA button fades in.
Product frame draws in from stroke animation.
Badge pops in with elastic scale.
Product micro-card slides up.

Suggested timing:

0.0s background fade
0.2s logo/menu reveal
0.4s headline reveal
0.6s drink image settle
0.9s CTA reveal
1.1s frame stroke draw
1.3s badge pop
1.4s product card reveal
Marquee Motion

The yellow-green curved text strips should move continuously.

Hero Ribbon

Motion:

Left-to-right infinite scroll along curved path

Speed:

20–35 seconds per loop

The movement should be slow enough to read but active enough to feel alive.

Promo Card Ribbon

The curved ribbon around the lifestyle image can move subtly upward/downward or rotate very slowly.

Possible motion:

rotate: -2deg to 2deg
duration: 8s
repeat: infinite
yoyo: true

7. Production Summary

This UI should be built as a premium mobile-first matcha eCommerce landing page with editorial visual composition, lifestyle-led product storytelling, and repeated purchase CTAs.

The strongest design elements are:

The hero drink-in-sky composition
The curved moving benefit ribbons
The cream and deep green contrast
The mix of sans-serif and italic serif typography
The product/lifestyle split card
The oversized footer wordmark

The final build should feel less like a standard café website and more like a social-first beverage campaign page designed for brand memorability and product conversion.
